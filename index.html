About Me

Hello, my name is Filipe Cancellier da Costa.

I was born and raised in Brazil, more specifically in the city of Urussanga, Santa Catarina. I consider myself a good and a simple person, in my free time you will probably find me watching a good movie, playing video games or studying coding.

My career, so far, is summarized in engineering jobs. I have over 7 year of experience as a engineer, mainly in the construction area. Throughout these years I gathered many soft skills, such as teamwork, leadership, problem solving and others. I was faced with many challenges during my career that always helped me to improve professionally, being one of the biggest ones to work in a different country with a different language and using different construction standards. In 2019 due to a crisis in Brazil, I tried to find something different to work with and as I have always enjoyed computer, games and technology so I thought "Why not coding?"

I started enrolling in online programming courses and started with Python. It was a good start as the language is quite simple to understand, but I wanted to see something more real to be shown on the screen, and my cousin, who is a front-end engineer, showed me web development and I decided to learn Javascript. Web development was amazing, I could create so many things and they were all working together ( JS, CSS , HTML ), and rendering on my screen like a real website. I really liked, even though I was doing only very basic code, but the limitless creativity that it gives to you to create anything just made me amazed. Since then, I have been teaching myself through online courses such as Udemy, Udacity, RocketSeat and some websites skills such as codewars and hackerrank.

In 2022 I enrolled for a Master's degree in computer science in London, to elevate my skills to a competitive level, however at the time I had a financial problem and had to suspend the course. After that I kept studying by myself, and in 2023 I became a father, which was a great surprise, but a very happy one, and because of that I had to think about my baby and decided to keep working as a Civil Engineer for a little more before trying to change my career.

It takes me to 2024, when I did a full stack web development bootcamp from HyperionDev, and learned even more about web, and also refreshed my mind for techologies I had already learned like, React, MongoDD, SQL, Ajax, Bootstrap, Express etc... And got myself motivated again to try to get my first role in the tech industry!
